Breaking the Vicious Cycle: Coherent 3D Gaussian Splatting from Sparse and Motion-Blurred View

Under Review by IEEE T-CSVT
Chaoran Feng*, Zhankuo Xu*, Yingtao Li, Jianbin Zhao, Jiashu Yang, Wangbo Yu,
Li Yuan, Yonghong Tian

School of Electronics and Computer Engineering, Peking University
Paper Appendix Video Code

Abstract


3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a state-of-the-art method for novel view synthesis. However, its performance heavily relies on dense, high-quality input imagery, an assumption that is often violated in real-world applications, where data is typically sparse and motion-blurred. These two issues create a vicious cycle: sparse views lack the multi-view constraints necessary to resolve motion blur, while the blur itself erases high-frequency details crucial for aligning the limited views. As a result, reconstruction often fails catastrophically, with fragmented views and a low-frequency bias. To break this cycle, we introduce CoherentGS, a novel framework for high-fidelity 3D reconstruction from sparse and blurry images. Our key insight is to address these compound degradations using a dual-prior strategy. We combine two pre-trained generative models: a specialized deblurring network for restoring sharp details and providing photometric guidance, and a diffusion model that offers geometric priors to fill in unobserved regions of the scene. This dual-prior strategy is supported by several key techniques, including a consistency-guided camera exploration module that adaptively guides the generative process, and a depth regularization loss that ensures geometric plausibility. We evaluate CoherentGS through both quantitative and qualitative experiments on synthetic and real-world scenes, using as few as 3, 6, and 9 input views. Our results demonstrate that CoherentGS significantly outperforms existing methods, setting a new state-of-the-art for this challenging task.


Method

The overview of our CoherentGS framework. It employs a dual-prior strategy for joint 3D reconstruction and enhancement. (Left) From sparse and blurry inputs, we first estimate initial camera poses and initialize a coarse 3DGS model. The model is then progressively refined through two parallel supervisory branches: (Top) Photometric Restoration via Deblurring Priors: This branch tackles motion blur. It explicitly models the camera's exposure trajectory to render a simulated blurry image, supervised against the real input via a reconstruction loss. Concurrently, it uses a pre-trained image deblurring model to create a sharp target, and a perceptual loss distills this deblurring knowledge into a rendered sharp view. (Bottom) Geometric Guidance via Repair Diffusion Priors: This branch addresses sparsity by exploring novel viewpoints in under-observed regions. A sharp image rendered from a new pose is enhanced by a powerful diffusion model, and a score distillation loss guides the 3DGS to align with this repaired view. Throughout the process, a depth smoothness loss is also applied to ensure geometric consistency.
